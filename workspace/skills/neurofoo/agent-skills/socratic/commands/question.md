# Probing Questions

Generate thought-provoking questions to examine a belief, idea, or claim more deeply.

## Instructions

Create questions across multiple categories that would help someone think more deeply about the topic. Focus on uncovering assumptions and exploring implications.

### Output Format

**Topic**: [What we're questioning]

---

## Clarifying Questions
*Getting clear on what's being claimed*

1. What do you mean by "[key term]"?
2. Can you give me a specific example?
3. How would you define [term] differently from [similar term]?

---

## Assumption Questions
*Surfacing hidden beliefs*

1. What would have to be true for this to be correct?
2. Why do you assume [X]?
3. Is [assumption] always true, or just sometimes?

---

## Evidence Questions
*Examining the basis for the claim*

1. How do you know this?
2. What evidence would change your mind?
3. Where does this information come from?

---

## Implication Questions
*Following the logic forward*

1. If this is true, then what else must be true?
2. What happens if we take this to its logical extreme?
3. How does this apply in [different context]?

---

## Counter Questions
*Considering alternative views*

1. What would someone who disagrees say?
2. Can you think of a case where the opposite is true?
3. What's the strongest argument against this?

---

## Meta Questions
*Questioning the question*

1. Why does this matter?
2. Is this the right question to be asking?
3. What are we really trying to understand?
