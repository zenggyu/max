# Eisenhower Matrix Examples

## Example 1: Product Manager's Week

**Context**: Product Manager, prioritizing this week's work

### Q1: DO (Urgent + Important)

| Task | Why Urgent? | Why Important? | Do By |
|------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Fix critical bug blocking launch | Customers affected now | Revenue impact | Today |
| Prepare board presentation | Meeting is Thursday | Funding decision | Wednesday |
| Respond to enterprise deal questions | Deal closing Friday | Largest customer | Today |

### Q2: SCHEDULE (Not Urgent + Important)

| Task | Why Important? | Schedule For | Time Needed |
|------|----------------|--------------|-------------|
| Q2 roadmap planning | Sets next 3 months direction | Tuesday 2-5pm | 3 hours |
| 1:1s with engineering leads | Relationship, context sharing | Wed/Thu mornings | 2 hours |
| Competitive analysis | Inform pricing strategy | Friday morning | 2 hours |
| Write product vision doc | Align team on direction | Next week | 4 hours |

### Q3: DELEGATE (Urgent + Not Important)

| Task | Why Urgent? | Who Can Handle? | Delegation Plan |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Update product demo | Sales meeting tomorrow | Solution Engineer | Share notes, they know product |
| Collect user feedback summary | Exec asked for it | UX Researcher | They have raw data |
| Fix typo in help docs | User complained | Support team | They have edit access |

### Q4: ELIMINATE (Not Urgent + Not Important)

| Task | Why Are You Doing This? | Elimination Strategy |
|------|-------------------------|---------------------|
| Attending all standups | "Staying informed" | Read async updates instead |
| Reading all Slack channels | FOMO | Unsubscribe from low-signal channels |
| Reformatting spreadsheets | Procrastination | Ship ugly; no one cares |

**Time Distribution**

| Quadrant | % of Time | Ideal % | Gap |
|----------|-----------|---------|-----|
| Q1: Do | 50% | 20-25% | -25% too much |
| Q2: Schedule | 20% | 60-70% | +40% needed |
| Q3: Delegate | 20% | 10-15% | -5% to delegate |
| Q4: Eliminate | 10% | <5% | -5% to cut |

**Insight**: Currently reactive (Q1 heavy). Need to delegate more and protect Q2 time.

## Example 2: Engineering Lead's Tasks

**Context**: Engineering lead, managing team + individual contributions

### Q1: DO

| Task | Why Urgent? | Why Important? | Do By |
|------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Production incident | Site is down | Customer trust | NOW |
| Unblock junior developer | They're stuck; sprint at risk | Sprint commitment | Today |
| Security patch | Vulnerability disclosed | Compliance | Today |

### Q2: SCHEDULE

| Task | Why Important? | Schedule For | Time Needed |
|------|----------------|--------------|-------------|
| Technical design for Q2 feature | Prevents rework later | Mon 2-4pm | 2 hours |
| Career conversations with reports | Retention, growth | 1:1 slots | 3 hours/week |
| Refactor auth module | Tech debt slowing every feature | Thu afternoon | 4 hours |
| Evaluate new monitoring tool | Prevent future incidents | Next week | 2 hours |

### Q3: DELEGATE

| Task | Why Urgent? | Who Can Handle? | Delegation Plan |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Code review backlog | PRs waiting | Senior engineer | Split reviews |
| On-call rotation schedule | Next month starts soon | Team collectively | Let them self-organize |
| Interview scheduling | Candidates waiting | Recruiter | They're better at it |

### Q4: ELIMINATE

| Task | Why Are You Doing This? | Elimination Strategy |
|------|-------------------------|---------------------|
| Attending every Zoom happy hour | Team bonding guilt | Attend 1 per month |
| Manually deploying to staging | "Always done it" | Automate via CI |
| Reading every PR comment | Control | Trust reviewers |

## Example 3: Founder's Monthly View

**Context**: Startup founder, monthly priorities

### Q1: DO

| Task | Why Urgent? | Why Important? | Do By |
|------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Close Series A term sheet | Investors have deadline | Company survival | Week 2 |
| Hire VP Sales | Pipeline growing; can't handle | Revenue growth | Week 3 |
| Sign strategic partnership | Partner has other options | Distribution | Week 1 |

### Q2: SCHEDULE

| Task | Why Important? | Schedule For | Time Needed |
|------|----------------|--------------|-------------|
| Define company values | Scaling culture | Week 2 | Half day |
| Board meeting prep | Governance, strategy | Week 4 | 1 day |
| Think time (unscheduled) | Strategic clarity | Weekly Friday PM | 3 hours |
| Customer visits | Ground truth | Week 3 | 2 days |

### Q3: DELEGATE

| Task | Why Urgent? | Who Can Handle? | Delegation Plan |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Office lease negotiation | Lease expiring | COO | Set parameters |
| Investor updates | Monthly due | Chief of Staff | Template + review |
| Conference speaking requests | Deadlines | EA | Accept/decline criteria |

### Q4: ELIMINATE

| Task | Why Are You Doing This? | Elimination Strategy |
|------|-------------------------|---------------------|
| Most LinkedIn requests | Politeness | Batch monthly; decline most |
| "Quick coffee" meetings | Hard to say no | Default no; office hours instead |
| Detailed expense reviews | Control | Trust finance team |

**Time Distribution Target**:
- Q1: 30% (higher than ideal due to fundraising)
- Q2: 50% (protect even during fundraise)
- Q3: 15% (ruthless delegation)
- Q4: 5% (say no to nearly everything)

## Example 4: Personal Life

**Context**: Work-life balance for a working parent

### Q1: DO

| Task | Why Urgent? | Why Important? | Do By |
|------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Pick up sick kid from school | They're sick now | Child wellbeing | Now |
| Pay mortgage | Due date | Shelter | Before 15th |
| Renew car registration | Expired | Legal | This week |

### Q2: SCHEDULE

| Task | Why Important? | Schedule For | Time Needed |
|------|----------------|--------------|-------------|
| Weekly date night | Relationship health | Saturday 7pm | 3 hours |
| Exercise | Physical/mental health | MWF 6am | 45 min |
| Read with kids | Connection, learning | Nightly 7:30pm | 30 min |
| Plan family vacation | Memories, anticipation | Sunday morning | 2 hours |

### Q3: DELEGATE

| Task | Why Urgent? | Who Can Handle? | Delegation Plan |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Lawn maintenance | Getting overgrown | Hire service | Monthly cost < time value |
| Grocery shopping | Need food | Delivery service | Instacart weekly |
| Kids' activity logistics | Practices, games | Carpool with other parents | Coordinate schedule |

### Q4: ELIMINATE

| Task | Why Are You Doing This? | Elimination Strategy |
|------|-------------------------|---------------------|
| Doom scrolling after kids sleep | Tired, default behavior | Phone in drawer at 9pm |
| Volunteer committee I dread | Guilt | Finish term, don't renew |
| Maintaining "perfect" lawn | Neighbor comparison | Lower standards; no one cares |

## Common Mistakes

**Everything in Q1**
If everything is urgent, nothing is. Ask: "What actually happens if this waits?"

**Empty Q2**
If you never schedule important-but-not-urgent work, you'll always be in Q1. Q2 is where you prevent future crises.

**Can't delegate (Q3)**
"No one can do it as well as me" = bottleneck. Train people or lower standards.

**Disguised Q4**
"Reading industry news" feels Q2 but is often Q4. Ask: "Did this change what I do?"
